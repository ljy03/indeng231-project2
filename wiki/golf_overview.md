# Golf

## Summary

Golf is a precision club-and-ball sport in which players use various clubs to hit balls into a series of holes on a course in as few strokes as possible. Originating in 15th-century Scotland, golf has grown into one of the world's most widely played sports, with millions of participants across more than 200 countries and a professional tour ecosystem spanning every continent. Unlike most sports, golf is played over natural and designed terrain, requiring players to adapt their strategy and technique to vastly different conditions on every shot.

## History and Origins

Golf's earliest documented history traces to 15th-century Scotland, where the game was played on coastal linksland — the sandy, windswept terrain between the sea and farmable land. The first written record of golf appears in a 1457 Scottish Parliament act that banned the game because it distracted men from archery practice. The oldest golf club in the world, The Honourable Company of Edinburgh Golfers, was founded in 1744, and the [[St Andrews Links]] Old Course has been played continuously since at least the 16th century.

The game spread to England, continental Europe, and eventually the Americas and Asia during the 18th and 19th centuries. The formation of The Royal and Ancient Golf Club of St Andrews in 1754 established early governance, and [[The Open Championship]] — the world's oldest golf major — was first played in 1860 at Prestwick Golf Club. Modern global governance is shared between the R&A and the United States Golf Association (USGA), which together maintain the official [[Rules of Golf]].

## How the Game Is Played

A standard round of golf consists of 18 holes, though nine-hole rounds are common. Each hole has a designated par — the number of strokes a skilled golfer is expected to take — typically ranging from par 3 to par 5. Players begin each hole on the teeing area and aim to complete the hole by sinking the ball into a cup (4.25 inches in diameter) cut into a putting green. The player with the fewest total strokes wins in stroke play, while match play compares hole-by-hole results.

Players carry a set of up to 14 clubs (as permitted by the rules), including drivers, fairway woods, irons, wedges, and a putter. Course lengths typically range from 6,000 to over 7,500 yards for championship layouts. Weather, terrain, elevation, and wind all significantly affect play, making course management and shot selection as important as raw ball-striking ability.

## Course Design and Terrain

Golf courses are designed to challenge players with varied terrain including fairways, rough, bunkers (sand traps), water hazards, and precisely maintained putting greens. Links courses, like [[St Andrews Links]], feature open, windswept ground with few trees and firm turf. Parkland courses, common in the United States, are set among trees and manicured grass. Desert and mountain layouts present entirely different strategic demands.

[[Augusta National Golf Club]], home of the [[Masters Tournament]], is regarded as one of the most beautifully designed courses in the world, with its sloping fairways, tiered greens, and strategic water features demanding precise shot-making at every hole.

## Professional Golf

The professional game is organized primarily around the PGA Tour (United States), the DP World Tour (Europe), and the Japan Golf Tour, among others. The four major championships — the [[Masters Tournament]], [[U.S. Open Golf]], [[The Open Championship]], and the [[PGA Championship]] — represent the sport's highest individual honors. Players who win all four majors during their career are said to have completed the Career Grand Slam, a feat achieved by only a handful of players including [[Tiger Woods]] and [[Rory McIlroy]].
